Advanced Measures of Central Tendency and Dispersion

Introduction

Descriptive statistics help summarise data effectively and provide insights into the central tendency and variability of datasets. This section focuses on advanced measures such as weighted mean, trimmed mean, and coefficient of variation (CV). Additionally, visualisations like density plots and boxplots are used to explore data distributions.

Weighted Mean

The Weighted Mean adjusts the average value by assigning weights to each data point. For example, sales can be weighted by store sizes to account for differences in store capacity. 


def calculate_weighted_mean(values, weights):
    return np.average(values, weights=weights)

weighted_mean_sales = calculate_weighted_mean(data["Sales"], data["StoreSize"])

Result: Weighted Mean Sales = {{weighted_mean_sales:.2f}}

Trimmed Mean

The Trimmed Mean removes a small percentage of the highest and lowest values, reducing the impact of outliers. This provides a more robust measure of central tendency. 


def calculate_trimmed_mean(values, trim_percent):
    trimmed_values = values.sort_values().iloc[int(len(values) * trim_percent):-int(len(values) * trim_percent)]
    return trimmed_values.mean()

trimmed_mean_sales = calculate_trimmed_mean(data["Sales"], trim_percent=0.05)

Result: Trimmed Mean Sales (5% trimmed) = {{trimmed_mean_sales:.2f}}

Coefficient of Variation (CV)

The Coefficient of Variation (CV) measures the relative variability of data as a percentage of the mean. It is useful for comparing variability across different datasets, such as sales and expenses. 


cv_sales = data["Sales"].std() / data["Sales"].mean() * 100
cv_expenses = data["Expenses"].std() / data["Expenses"].mean() * 100

Results:

  • Sales CV: {{cv_sales:.2f}}%
  • Expenses CV: {{cv_expenses:.2f}}%

Conclusion

Advanced descriptive measures like weighted mean and trimmed mean provide deeper insights into central tendencies, while the coefficient of variation highlights variability in data. Visualisations complement these metrics, enabling a comprehensive understanding of data distributions and relationships.
