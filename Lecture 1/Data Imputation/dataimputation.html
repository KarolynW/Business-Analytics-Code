Data Imputation Task - Olympus Manufacturing

Scenario

Olympus Manufacturing, a UK-based company, operates two factories that produce industrial components for various industries such as automotive, construction, and consumer goods. Their manufacturing process involves sourcing raw materials, assembling components, conducting quality control checks, and distributing products to suppliers and businesses.

Recently, data analysts at Olympus Manufacturing have identified **missing values** in critical operational datasets. These missing values create inefficiencies in production tracking, quality control, and supply chain management. Your task is to detect, analyse, and handle missing data using appropriate **data imputation techniques**.

Task: Imputing Missing Data

You have been given a sample dataset (olympus_manufacturing_data.xlsx), which includes:

  • Raw Material Inventory: Data on material levels across both factories.
  • Production Line Efficiency: Records of assembly times and component production rates.
  • Quality Control Reports: Inspection results indicating whether components meet required standards.
  • Distribution Logs: Records of finished product shipments from factories to clients.

However, some key data points are missing, affecting:

  • Inventory records: Some materials are missing stock level data, leading to supply chain disruptions.
  • Production efficiency metrics: Some assembly times and defect rates are missing, affecting efficiency analysis.
  • Quality control reports: Missing inspection results make it difficult to track manufacturing errors.
  • Distribution tracking: Some shipments lack status updates, impacting delivery accuracy.

Your Task

Your objective is to apply **data imputation techniques** using Python or R to **handle missing values** in the dataset. Specifically, you need to:

  • Load the dataset into Python or R.
  • Identify missing values and summarise them (count & percentage).
  • Choose an appropriate imputation technique based on the dataset structure.
  • Implement imputation using **at least two methods**, such as:
    • Mean/Median Imputation for numerical data.
    • K-Nearest Neighbour (KNN) Imputation for structured data.
    • Multiple Imputation for datasets requiring more robust handling.
  • Visualise missing data before and after imputation using heatmaps and bar charts.
  • Validate the imputation by comparing distributions and correlations before and after filling missing values.
  • Save and download all analysis outputs, including summaries, imputed datasets, and visualisations.

Hints for Students

  • Use pandas in Python or dplyr in R to explore the dataset.
  • For visualization, use **seaborn** in Python or **ggplot2** in R.
  • Use the **sklearn KNNImputer** in Python or **VIM::kNN** in R for KNN imputation.
  • Compare **summary statistics before and after imputation** to evaluate accuracy.
  • Check if relationships between variables have changed after imputation.
